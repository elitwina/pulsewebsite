Quick guide

From zero to your first Pulse-assisted set in under five minutes. Works alongside Serato, Rekordbox, Traktor, VirtualDJ or djay Pro - it never touches your library, crates or files.

1

Download Pulse

Grab the right build for your machine. Free to start, no credit card.

macOS DMG  ·  Windows (Microsoft Store)

2

Install & sign in

Open the installer and follow the prompts. On first launch, create a free account with your email - that's the only personally identifiable info Pulse asks for. Signing in on a second laptop pulls your history with you.

3

Open your DJ software

Start Serato, Rekordbox, Traktor, VirtualDJ or djay Pro the way you normally would. Pulse runs as a separate, always-on-top window - you don't change anything in your DJ app.

4

Drop a track in your deck

Load any track in your DJ app. Pulse sees it instantly and serves the next three that work after it, ranked by what other DJs (and your own history) actually play next. No alt-tabbing - just glance at the Pulse Window.

5

Let MyStyle catch up

Within 15-20 minutes, Pulse finishes scanning your set history and starts blending in your patterns - the tracks you play after a given record, including ones you'd forgotten. Toggle MyStyle on/off any time.

6

Switch engines as the night moves

Tap once to flip between four recommendation engines:

  • Popular - most-played combinations all-time
  • By Style - closest musical-style matches
  • Recent - what other DJs played this week
  • Recent Weighted - recent with a freshness bias for new releases
7

Open it up to your room (optional)

Create an event in Pulse and you get a QR code. Print it, project it, share it - guests scan and send requests. The ones that fit what you're playing right now glow in the Pulse Window. The rest sit quietly.

8

After the set

When the lights come up, Pulse writes you a Set Report - BPM curve, genre mix, track count, your stats vs every other Pulse DJ, plus a short AI summary of how the set actually flowed. Export the playlist for promoters, Insta, or just memory.

Things worth knowing on day one

  • Internet at the gig? Only the first hour, while Pulse caches a local DB. After that the room's Wi-Fi can drop and Pulse keeps suggesting.
  • Does Pulse touch my library? No. It runs alongside your DJ app and never modifies your crates, playlists or files.
  • Multi-laptop? Sign in on each machine. One account, history follows you everywhere.
  • Languages. English, Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Hebrew. Switch any time.
  • Privacy. Email + password is all Pulse asks for. Set data feeds the model anonymously - no other DJ ever sees what you played. Full details in the Privacy Policy.
